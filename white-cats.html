    • Characteristics and physical attributes of White cats

    White cats can have dense, silky, short, or wavy coats, with striking eye colors such as blue, green, gold, or odd eyes (one blue, one gold). Some may have rounded features, while others appear sleek and agile. Personality traits vary—some breeds are gentle and quiet, like Persians, while others are playful and energetic, such as Cornish Rex or Turkish Van cats. Some white cats with blue eyes may have a genetic predisposition to deafness.

    Should you get an White cats?

    White cats are undeniably beautiful, often having striking eye colors like blue, green, or gold. Some breeds, like Turkish Angoras or Maine Coons, have gorgeous fluffy coats, while others, like Siamese or Cornish Rex, have sleeker appearances. However, there are some things to consider. White cats, especially those with blue eyes, can have a higher chance of deafness due to genetics. This doesn’t mean they can’t live happy, fulfilling lives—it just requires some adjustments in communication and care. Their pristine coats also tend to highlight dirt more, so they may need extra grooming to keep them looking their best. Ultimately, if you love the idea of a white cat and are prepared to care for their needs, they can be a fantastic choice
